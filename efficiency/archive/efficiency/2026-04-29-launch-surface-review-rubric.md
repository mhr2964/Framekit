# Framekit Launch-Surface Review Rubric
_Date: 2026-04-29_

Use this sheet to review and refine three launch-surface artifact types in parallel:
1. landing-page positioning
2. waitlist CTA copy
3. screenshot / social asset direction

Goal: make selection faster and more objective while preserving Framekit’s clarity-first positioning.

---

## 1) Core evaluation criteria

Score each option from **1–5** on the four criteria below.

### A. Clarity
**Question:** Can a first-time visitor understand what Framekit is, who it is for, and why it matters within a few seconds?

**High-score signals**
- Plain language over slogans
- Specific user/problem context
- Minimal ambiguity
- Can be paraphrased easily after one read

**Low-score signals**
- Clever but vague phrasing
- Multiple ideas competing in one line
- Missing user or use case
- Requires product context to decode

### B. Differentiation
**Question:** Does this option make Framekit feel distinct from generic collaboration, PM, or feedback tools?

**High-score signals**
- Highlights calm review rooms, polished delivery, timestamped website feedback
- Implies “less chaos than email/Slack/calls/bloated suites”
- Shows a focused wedge, not a broad software category claim

**Low-score signals**
- Could fit any SaaS tool
- Sounds like project management or generic team collaboration
- Uses category clichés without a Framekit-specific angle

### C. Credibility
**Question:** Does the promise feel believable based on the current product and likely first release?

**High-score signals**
- Matches the actual create-room/review flow
- Uses concrete, supportable claims
- Avoids inflated transformation language
- Can be reinforced by product screenshots or prototype states

**Low-score signals**
- Overpromises automation, scale, or features not in scope
- Relies on abstract emotional payoff alone
- Creates expectations the current product cannot satisfy

### D. Conversion intent
**Question:** Does this option create a clear next step and motivate the target user to take it?

**High-score signals**
- CTA is specific and low-friction
- Value is tied to action
- Copy creates urgency, relevance, or curiosity without hype
- Asset direction naturally leads toward signup or learn-more behavior

**Low-score signals**
- Passive or generic CTA
- No clear next action
- Value proposition disconnected from action
- Asset is attractive but not persuasive

### Decision rule
- **16–20:** strong candidate; refine only
- **12–15:** viable but needs focused revision
- **8–11:** unclear or weak; major rewrite
- **<8:** reject and replace

Tie-breaker order:
1. clarity
2. differentiation
3. credibility
4. conversion intent

If two options tie, choose the one that is easier to support with the current product flow.

## 3) Surface-specific review prompts

### Landing-page positioning
- Does the headline say what the product is, not just how it feels?
- Is the target buyer implied or named?
- Is the anti-chaos / anti-bloat wedge visible?
- Can the supporting line connect directly to the actual create-room and review experience?

### Waitlist CTA copy
- Is the action explicit?
- Does the user know what they are getting access to?
- Is the reward concrete enough to justify signup now?
- Is the CTA tone aligned with Framekit: calm, polished, useful?

### Screenshot / social asset direction
- Is the visual idea anchored in a real product moment?
- Would the caption still make sense if seen out of context?
- Does the concept show a benefit quickly?
- Does it imply “this is cleaner/easier than the messy way I do it now”?

## 4) Rapid iteration checklist

Use this after each review pass.

### From draft to revision
- Remove any phrase that could describe a generic SaaS product
- Replace abstract wording with user/problem-specific wording
- Ensure one primary idea per headline or asset
- Check alignment with current product scope and prototype
- Confirm the CTA asks for one clear action only

### From revision to selection
- Compare top options side by side in the matrix
- Keep the option with the strongest clarity score unless differentiation is materially better elsewhere
- Rewrite only the weakest line, not the whole concept, when the structure is sound
- Preserve message consistency across headline, CTA, and asset angle

### From selection to ready-for-launch brief
- Lock the chosen headline / CTA / asset concept in one sentence each
- Add target audience and intended takeaway
- Add required proof/support elements (UI state, screenshot, product moment, subhead)
- Note any claims that must not be made yet
- Hand off only options marked **Keep** or high-confidence **Revise**

---

## 5) Recommended working cadence
For each launch-surface round:
1. draft 2–3 options per surface
2. score all options quickly
3. reject weak/generic options immediately
4. revise only the top 1–2
5. lock one winner per surface for packaging

This keeps growth moving without over-polishing low-potential concepts.
